hi,i'm

A sophomore at Brown University studying Computer Science and Neuroscience with a focus on applications of artificial intelligence and software engineering to healthcare. I have experience building applications with Java, Python, Typescript, HTML, CSS, and a few other languages.

I believe that technology will become increasingly relevant in the future, especially in the field of medicine. As such, software developers must not only write clean and efficient code, but also think about the implications of technologies. For example, AI might introduce aspects of bias to medical imaging. I believe that my computational background will prepare me to address these challenging considerations.

This website contains information about me, my projects, and the organizations I am involved with.

about me

I grew up in Phoenix, Arizona before I moved to the Bay Area when I was in 3rd grade. I became interested in medicine at a young age through personal experiences with the healthcare system. My passion for medicine grew in high school and as an undergraduate through my clinical experiences volunteering at local hospitals and shadowing physicians. Another one of my interests is computer science. My fascination with computer science and technology developed in high school when I took my school's AP Computer Science course and had the opportunity to experiment with my own Raspberry PI. I am working to combine these interests by building software that further integrates technology with medicine.

I am comfortable with Java, Python, HTML, CSS, Typescript, C++, and C. Using this knowledge, I have built websites, complex AI models for tasks such as playing Paxcon and classifying images, and solved real world problems.
